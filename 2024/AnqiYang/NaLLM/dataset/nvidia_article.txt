NVIDIA (NASDAQ: NVDA) announced its financial results for the fourth quarter and fiscal year 2023. The company reported a quarterly revenue of $6.05 billion, which represents a 21% decrease from the previous year but a 2% increase from the previous quarter. The fiscal-year revenue remained stable at $27.0 billion. Quarterly and annual returns to shareholders amounted to $1.15 billion and $10.44 billion, respectively. For the fourth quarter ending January 29, 2023, GAAP earnings per diluted share were $0.57, a 52% decrease from the previous year but a 111% increase from the previous quarter. Non-GAAP earnings per diluted share were $0.88, down 33% year-over-year but up 52% from the previous quarter. For fiscal 2023, GAAP earnings per diluted share were $1.74, a 55% decline from the previous year, and non-GAAP earnings per diluted share were $3.34, down 25%.
Jensen Huang, founder and CEO of NVIDIA, highlighted the growing interest and adoption of AI across various industries, emphasizing the company's readiness to support this trend with its new AI supercomputer and gaming advancements. NVIDIA is also partnering with leading cloud service providers to offer AI-as-a-service, allowing enterprises to access its AI platform, including the NVIDIA DGX™ AI supercomputer and customizable AI models like NeMo™ and BioNeMo™.
During the fourth quarter of fiscal 2023, NVIDIA returned $1.15 billion to shareholders through share repurchases and cash dividends, bringing the fiscal year total to $10.44 billion. The company announced a quarterly cash dividend of $0.04 per share, payable on March 29, 2023.
In summary, NVIDIA's fourth quarter and fiscal year 2023 financial results reflect a mix of challenges and growth opportunities. Despite a decline in year-over-year revenue and earnings, the company showed quarter-over-quarter improvement and maintained strong fiscal year revenue. With significant advancements in AI and strategic partnerships, NVIDIA is well-positioned for future growth.
